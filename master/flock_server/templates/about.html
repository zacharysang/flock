About

Learn the basics about Flock. What the whole point is, how to get started, and more!

What is Flock?

Flock is a platform-as-a-service for distributed and volunteer computing projects. Flock makes it easier for you to build, host, monitor, and find workers for your distributed computing applications. Programs on the Flock platform are run entirely in the browser, and built based on the OpenMPI standard implemented in our JavaScript-based MPI libraries. Flock also provides automated hosting and project monitoring for all distributed computing projects on the platform.

Why the Web?

Running projects in the web offers better security and accessibility than native distributed computing applications. Web browsers have built-in sandboxing that assures volunteers it is safe for them to run your programs on their devices. Web-based volunteering means that your volunteers don't need to worry about installing software to help run your distributed computing program - all they need to do is leave a browser tab open on your project URL, and their browser will automatically begin working on your project. Web-based projects are significantly more accessible, and can even be run on volunteers' cell phones!
